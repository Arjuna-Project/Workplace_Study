Sales & Marketing

Connecting products with customers and driving company growth.

They handle clients, promote company products, and ensure the solutions offered match the customer's needs.

Core duties involve direct sales and marketing execution:

  • Handling client calls, product demonstrations, and follow-ups.
  • Planning and supporting marketing campaigns.
  • Monitoring sales performance and preparing reports.

Supporting tasks include:

  • Assisting with market research.
  • Collecting customer feedback for the product team.
  • Maintaining CRM (Customer Relationship Management) data.
  • Coordinating with other teams for events or product updates.

The reporting structure is layered, moving up from a manager to the head of the department:

Line: Sales Executive → Team Lead → Sales Manager → Senior Manager → Head of Sales and Marketing

Many start in a general sales role. To move into the IT sector, they often learn digital marketing and CRM tools through online courses, then transition into a software company.

Growth depends on performance and continuous learning:

Path: Sales Executive → Senior Executive → Team Lead → Manager → Business Development Head or Product Marketing Manager

A business degree like BBA is a common foundation. This is often supplemented with certifications in digital marketing and CRM tools to understand analytics and platforms.

They coordinate regularly with the Product, Development, and Customer Support teams. They are responsible for sharing client insights, feedback on features, and campaign reports.

Their toolkit includes:

  • CRM Tools (like Salesforce or HubSpot)
  • Google Analytics (for tracking campaigns)
  • Canva (for creating marketing content)
  • Excel / Google Sheets (for reporting and analysis)

They handle issues related to customer expectations and campaign performance. This includes clients changing their requirements, or analyzing data when sales numbers don't match expectations.

Someone who enjoys talking to people, solving problems, and handling dynamic situations. The role requires patience, confidence, and a curiosity to learn new tools and understand customer behavior.
