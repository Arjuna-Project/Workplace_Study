HR & Talent Acquisition (TA)

The "People Managers" and "Culture Custodians" of the company.

They manage the entire employee lifecycle. Their primary duties include:

  • Talent Acquisition: Sourcing, interviewing, and hiring new talent.
  • Onboarding: Integrating new hires into the company.
  • Employee Relations: Managing performance reviews and resolving conflicts.
  • HR Operations: Handling attendance, leave, payroll inputs, and legal compliance (like POSH).

Typically, they report to an HR Manager or Head of People/HR Director. In smaller companies, they might report directly to the COO or CEO.

Most professionals start as an HR Assistant, HR Intern, or Junior Recruiter. This provides a foundation in recruitment, data management, and operational processes.

The path moves from administrative roles to strategic management:

Path: HR Intern → HR Executive → HR Generalist / TA Specialist → HR Manager → Head of HR → Chief People Officer (CPO)

  • Degree: Bachelor's in HR, Business, or Psychology is standard.
  • Advanced: An MBA in HR is highly valued for senior roles.
  • Certifications: SHRM or PHR are valuable extras.

HR is a central hub. They work with:

  • Technical Teams: To define job requirements and plan hiring.
  • Finance: To coordinate payroll, compensation, and budgets.
  • Legal: To ensure compliance with labor laws.
  • Marketing: To support employer branding to attract talent.
  • HRIS (Info Systems): Workday, BambooHR, Keka, Zoho People.
  • ATS (Tracking): Greenhouse, Lever, Taleo.
  • Sourcing: LinkedIn Recruiter, Naukri, Indeed.
  • General: MS Excel/Google Sheets, Slack/Teams.

They balance employee needs with business needs, including:

  • Resolving team conflicts and sensitive employee grievances.
  • Hiring for highly specialized technical roles.
  • Reducing employee turnover and preventing burnout.
  • Aligning salary expectations with the company's budget.

Someone who is a strong, empathetic listener, highly organized, diplomatic, and can maintain confidentiality in sensitive situations.

Hiring and supporting skilled, motivated people ensures the company can deliver quality products. A positive culture leads to engaged employees, which directly improves customer satisfaction.

AI and HR systems automate routine admin tasks (like resume screening). This frees HR to focus on high-value, human-centric activities like strategy, culture, and relationship building.

Point Talent Acquisition (TA) Human Resources (HR)
Main Role Hires new people for the company. Manages employees after they join.
Focus Recruitment and talent sourcing. Employee care, training, salary, policies.
Work Stage Before joining. After joining.
Goal Find the best people for open jobs. Keep employees happy and productive.

Easy Line to Remember:

"TA brings people in, HR helps them stay and grow."
