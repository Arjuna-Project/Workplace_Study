IT Support

The technical backbone of the workplace, keeping all systems running smoothly.

IT Support helps employees solve technical problems and keeps company systems running. Their duties include:

  • Primary: Fixing hardware/software, resetting passwords, managing user accounts (Active Directory, Azure), handling network/VPN issues, and setting up laptops for new joiners.
  • Secondary: Documenting solutions, creating knowledge articles, and assisting with IT projects or system upgrades.

They usually report to an IT Support Team Lead or Service Desk Manager. They also work closely with Network, Hardware, and Application teams for escalations.

Most start in entry-level roles like Helpdesk Support or Service Desk Analyst (L1). At this stage, they focus on basic tasks like password resets, software installation, and connectivity problems.

The path moves from general support to specialized and leadership roles:

Path: L1 Support Analyst → L2 Technical Support / System Admin → L3 Infrastructure / Network / Cloud Engineer → IT Team Lead → IT Manager

A degree or diploma in Computer Science / IT is standard. Optional certifications like CompTIA A+, Network+, CCNA, or Microsoft/AWS certs are very helpful.

They are a central hub. They escalate complex issues to L2/L3 teams, collaborate with Network and Security teams on access or VPN issues, and coordinate with Hardware/Asset Management for repairs.

Their daily toolkit includes:

  • Ticketing: ServiceNow, Jira, Freshworks
  • Admin: Active Directory, Azure AD, Intune
  • Remote Support: AnyDesk, TeamViewer, Microsoft Teams
  • Monitoring: Zabbix, SolarWinds
  • Security: FortiClient VPN, Defender
  • Communication: Teams, Outlook, Slack

They fix all the technical issues that stop employees from working. This includes hardware and software failures, password resets and account lockouts, network or Wi-Fi problems, and VPN connectivity issues.

This job is perfect for someone who is calm, patient, and enjoys solving technical challenges. They must be a good communicator who can explain technical ideas to non-technical users and have a genuine desire to help people.

They ensure smooth business operations. By fixing issues quickly, they reduce downtime, improve employee productivity, and keep the company's technology secure and reliable.

The role is now more proactive. AI handles routine tasks like password resets and system monitoring, which reduces manual work. This allows the IT team to focus on improving performance, ensuring security, and preventing issues before they happen.
